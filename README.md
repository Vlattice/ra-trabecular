# RA-Trabecular

**RA-Trabecular** is a computational framework for modeling trabecular bone degradation in rheumatoid arthritis (RA) as a percolation-driven loss of mechanical connectivity.

The project integrates:

* Voronoi tessellation–based trabecular network generation
* Percolation theory
* Finite Element Analysis (FEM)
* Structural connectivity metrics
* Anisotropy analysis
* Predictive modeling of mechanical failure

Rather than focusing exclusively on bone density or erosion volume, RA-Trabecular investigates how progressive degradation of trabecular connectivity affects the load-bearing competence of cancellous bone.

The framework introduces the **Index of Effective Mechanical Connectivity (IRCE)**, a dimensionless metric designed to quantify the transition from a mechanically competent trabecular network to a fragmented and structurally compromised state.

* Progressive erosion simulation
* Mechanical competence estimation
* Connectivity analysis
* Local anisotropy computation
* IRCE calculation
* Critical transition detection

---

## Research Goals

The project aims to explore whether trabecular degradation can be described as a topological and mechanical phase transition rather than solely as a reduction in bone mass.

Potential applications include:

* Rheumatoid arthritis
* Osteoporosis
* Psoriatic arthritis
* Subchondral osteoarthritis
* Computational bone biomechanics
* Structural failure prediction
